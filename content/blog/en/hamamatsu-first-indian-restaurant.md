# Hamamatsu's First Indian Restaurant: The Kumar Story Since 1995

In the heart of Hamamatsu, on the basement level of Act Tower, a restaurant has been serving authentic Indian cuisine for nearly three decades. Kumar Restaurant isn't just another dining spot—it's a story of cultural bridge-building, culinary passion, and one family's determination to bring the flavors of Uttar Pradesh to Shizuoka Prefecture.

If you've ever wondered how Hamamatsu ended up with one of the finest Indian restaurants in the region, this is the story behind it.

## A Dream Born in India

Every great restaurant begins with a person who believes in the power of food. For [Kumar Restaurant](/about), that person is Mr. Binay Kumar. Born and raised in Uttar Pradesh, one of India's most culinarily rich states, Mr. Kumar grew up surrounded by the aromas of freshly ground spices, slow-cooked curries, and tandoori breads pulled straight from clay ovens.

Uttar Pradesh cuisine is often overshadowed in international discussions by the more familiar Punjabi or South Indian traditions, but those who know it understand its depth. The cuisine balances bold flavors with subtle complexity—dishes like Awadhi biryani, which requires hours of layering rice with marinated meat, or the slow-cooked kormas that define Lucknow's royal culinary heritage.

Mr. Kumar didn't just carry recipes from his home; he carried a philosophy. In his family, cooking wasn't a chore—it was an act of love, a way to bring people together. When he decided to bring authentic Indian cuisine to Japan, he knew he couldn't take shortcuts. The spices had to be real. The techniques had to be traditional. The experience had to transport diners, even if just for an hour, to the bustling kitchens of northern India.

His wife shared his vision. Together, they envisioned a restaurant where Japanese guests could experience true Indian flavors—not the adapted, sweeter versions that had become common in some international Indian restaurants. They wanted people to taste what curry was supposed to taste like.

## Opening Day in 1995

The mid-1990s were a challenging time to start an international restaurant in Hamamatsu. The city was still recovering from the economic bubble, and Indian cuisine was largely unknown outside of Tokyo and Osaka. Finding the right ingredients posed the first major hurdle.

Many of the spices that form the backbone of Indian cooking simply weren't available in Japanese markets at the time. Cardamom, fenugreek, and specific varieties of chili peppers had to be sourced from specialty importers. Some ingredients were flown directly from India. Over time, Mr. Kumar built relationships with suppliers who could deliver consistent, high-quality spices—a network that Kumar Restaurant maintains to this day.

But the biggest challenge was cultural. In 1995, most Japanese diners had limited exposure to Indian food. The concept of a curry that wasn't Japanese curry—thick, sweet, and mild—was foreign to many. Mr. Kumar spent countless evenings explaining dishes, offering samples, and patiently helping guests navigate an unfamiliar menu.

Those early customers became the foundation of something remarkable. Word spread slowly but steadily. People who tried Kumar's butter chicken came back. They brought friends. They brought family. The restaurant earned its reputation one plate at a time.

The first months tested the team's resolve. There were quiet nights when only a handful of tables were occupied. But Mr. Kumar never wavered. He believed that if people tasted genuine Indian food, made with care and authenticity, they would return. He was right.

## Growing with Hamamatsu

Kumar Restaurant's decision to locate at Act Tower was strategic. The building is one of Hamamatsu's most recognizable landmarks—a 153-meter-tall tower that dominates the city skyline. Its central location made it accessible to both business lunch crowds and families looking for dinner.

But the real magic happened outside the restaurant's four walls. Mr. Kumar and his team became active members of the Hamamatsu community. They participated in local events, supported neighborhood initiatives, and built genuine relationships with the people around them.

One of the most fascinating aspects of Kumar's evolution has been how the menu adapted to Japanese tastes without sacrificing authenticity. Indian cuisine in Japan often undergoes significant modification—curries become sweeter, spice levels drop dramatically, and meat-heavy dishes dominate. Kumar Restaurant took a different approach.

The restaurant introduced spice level options, allowing guests to choose their heat level. Dishes were presented in ways that felt approachable to Japanese diners while maintaining the complex spice profiles that define genuine Indian cooking. The result was a menu that felt both familiar and exciting—a bridge between two culinary worlds.

Over the years, Kumar Restaurant expanded its offerings beyond the standard curry-and-rice format. Tandoori dishes, freshly baked naan, and specialty desserts like gulab jamun became signature items. Each addition was carefully considered, ensuring it met Mr. Kumar's standards for authenticity.

The restaurant also became a gathering place for Hamamatsu's growing international community. Japanese professionals who had traveled to India, Indian students studying in Shizuoka, and visitors from around the world found in Kumar Restaurant a taste of home. For many, it was the first place where they felt the warmth of Indian hospitality in Japan.

## From Local Gem to G7 Summit Caterer

The moment that truly put Kumar Restaurant on the international stage came in 2023, when the restaurant was selected to cater for world leaders at the G7 Summit in Kyoto. This wasn't a coincidence—it was the culmination of nearly three decades of consistent excellence.

The Indian government recognized Kumar Restaurant as a representative of authentic Indian cuisine in Japan. When world leaders gathered for the summit, the restaurant was entrusted with providing dishes that would showcase India's culinary heritage on the global stage. For Mr. Kumar and his team, it was both an honor and a responsibility they took extremely seriously.

Preparing for the G7 was a massive undertaking. The team developed special menus that highlighted the breadth of Indian cuisine—from delicate starters to rich, complex main courses. Every spice blend was checked and double-checked. Every dish was tested multiple times. The goal was perfection, because the world was watching.

The success at the G7 Summit validated everything Mr. Kumar had built. It proved that authentic Indian cuisine, prepared with integrity and skill, could hold its own on any stage. It also brought international recognition to Hamamatsu—a city not typically associated with fine dining.

Local media covered the story extensively. Suddenly, the restaurant at Act Tower B1 was no longer just a local favorite—it was a point of civic pride. Residents who had been dining at Kumar for years felt vindicated. They had always known the food was special; now the world knew it too.

## Our Two Locations Today

Today, Kumar Restaurant operates two locations in the Hamamatsu area, each with its own character.

**Act Tower B1 (Main Location)**

The original location at Act Tower B1 remains the flagship. Located beneath one of Hamamatsu's tallest buildings, it offers an intimate dining experience with seating for both small groups and larger parties. The menu here features the full range of Kumar's offerings—from classic curries to tandoori specialties, from vegetarian delights to rich meat dishes.

The atmosphere strikes a balance between traditional and contemporary. Warm lighting, carefully selected decor, and the ever-present aroma of spices create an environment that feels both special and comfortable. It's equally suited for a business lunch, a family dinner, or a romantic evening out.

**Kumar-san's Curry, Hamakita**

The second location in the Hamakita area brings Kumar's culinary philosophy to a different part of the city. This location has developed its own loyal following, particularly among residents who prefer a more casual setting.

Kumar-san's Curry focuses on accessible, everyday Indian dishes that can be enjoyed quickly. It's the perfect spot for a lunch break or a casual dinner with friends. The core recipes are the same ones that made the original location famous, adapted for a faster-service format.

Both locations share Mr. Kumar's commitment to fresh spices, authentic techniques, and genuine hospitality. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a longtime regular, the experience is designed to make you feel welcome and satisfied.

## What Sets Kumar Apart

In a world where Indian restaurants have become increasingly common, what makes Kumar Restaurant different? The answer lies in consistency and authenticity.

Mr. Kumar grinds spices fresh every day. This isn't a marketing gimmick—it's a practice that fundamentally changes the flavor of the food. Pre-ground spices lose their aromatic oils within hours of grinding. By preparing spice blends fresh each morning, Kumar Restaurant ensures that every dish reaches the table at its flavorful best.

The restaurant also maintains a direct connection to its culinary roots. Mr. Kumar regularly updates the menu with dishes that reflect the evolving food scene in Uttar Pradesh, while keeping the classics that loyal customers love. It's a living menu—one that honors tradition while embracing innovation.

Perhaps most importantly, the team at Kumar Restaurant genuinely cares about the experience. From the moment you walk through the door to the last bite of dessert, every detail matters. The staff is trained not just in food service, but in the philosophy of Indian hospitality—the idea that a guest is a gift, and their comfort is paramount.
