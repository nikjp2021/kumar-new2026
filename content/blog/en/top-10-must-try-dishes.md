# Top 10 Must-Try Dishes at Kumar Restaurant Hamamatsu

With over 25 years of serving authentic Indian cuisine in Hamamatsu, Kumar Restaurant has earned a reputation for dishes that consistently delight. Whether you're a regular who visits weekly or someone considering their first Indian meal, our menu offers something for every palate.

We asked our kitchen team to help us narrow down the dishes that best represent what we do. These are the ten plates that define Kumar Restaurant—each one prepared with freshly ground spices, time-honored techniques, and the care that Mr. Binay Kumar has insisted on since day one.

## #1 — Butter Chicken (Chicken Butter Masala)

**Price: ¥1,300**

If there's one dish that introduces people to Indian cuisine, it's butter chicken. But not all butter chicken is created equal. At Kumar Restaurant, our version starts with tender chicken pieces marinated overnight in a blend of yogurt and spices, then cooked in our tandoor oven until lightly charred. The chicken is then simmered in a rich, creamy tomato sauce infused with butter, fenugreek, and our house spice blend.

What sets our butter chicken apart is the balance. The sauce is rich without being heavy, sweet without being cloying, and spiced without overwhelming the palate. It's the dish that first-time visitors order and longtime customers return to again and again.

**Best paired with:** Garlic naan or steamed basmati rice.

## #2 — Chana Masala

**Price: ¥1,000**

Chana masala is a vegetarian classic that proves Indian cuisine doesn't need meat to be deeply satisfying. Our version features chickpeas slow-cooked in a robust tomato-based sauce with onions, ginger, garlic, and a carefully calibrated blend of spices.

The beauty of chana masala lies in its heartiness. The chickpeas absorb the flavors of the sauce, creating a dish that's protein-rich, warming, and endlessly comforting. At Kumar, we adjust the spice level to suit your preference—from mild to a more traditional heat that brings a gentle tingle to your lips.

This is one of our most popular dishes among vegetarian diners, but plenty of meat-eaters order it too. It's simply that good.

**Best paired with:** Bhatura (fried bread) or plain naan.

## #3 — Palak Paneer

**Price: ¥1,200**

Palak paneer is where nutrition meets indulgence. Fresh spinach is blanched and pureed, then cooked with cubes of paneer—a soft, fresh Indian cottage cheese—in a sauce that balances earthy greens with warm spices.

At Kumar, we don't over-process our spinach. You'll notice a slight texture that reminds you this is a real vegetable dish, not a homogeneous paste. The paneer is made fresh in-house, and the difference is immediately apparent. It's softer, creamier, and absorbs the surrounding flavors beautifully.

For diners looking for a dish that's both healthy and deeply flavorful, palak paneer is an excellent choice. It's also naturally gluten-free, making it a great option for guests with dietary restrictions.

**Best paired with:** Jeera rice (cumin rice) or tandoori roti.

## #4 — Keema Curry

**Price: ¥1,100**

Keema curry is comfort food at its finest. Minced lamb is cooked with onions, tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and a blend of warm spices until deeply flavorful and satisfying. The result is a dish with a rich, meaty sauce that coats every grain of rice it touches.

Our keema curry traces its roots to the keema dishes of Uttar Pradesh, where Mr. Binay Kumar grew up eating versions of this recipe at family gatherings. The spice blend is slightly different from what you'll find in restaurants that follow Punjabi traditions—it's more aromatic, with layers of flavor that reveal themselves as you eat.

For those who enjoy dishes with a bit more complexity, keema curry is an excellent exploration of Indian spice mastery.

**Best paired with:** Tandoori roti or paratha.

## #5 — Tandoori Chicken

**Price: ¥1,500**

The tandoor oven is the heart of any Indian kitchen, and tandoori chicken is its most iconic offering. Whole chicken pieces are marinated in yogurt, lemon juice, and a proprietary spice blend, then roasted in our clay tandoor at temperatures exceeding 400°C.

The result is chicken with a beautifully charred exterior and juicy, tender interior. The yogurt marinade tenderizes the meat while the high heat of the tandoor creates those distinctive smoky flavors that you simply can't replicate in a conventional oven.

At Kumar, our tandoori chicken is served with sliced onions, lemon wedges, and green chutney. It works beautifully as a main course or as a sharing appetizer for the table.

**Best paired with:** Roomali roti or a simple green salad.

## #6 — Samosa

**Price: ¥500**

The samosa is India's most beloved appetizer, and for good reason. Our version features a crispy, golden pastry shell filled with spiced potatoes, peas, and onions. Each samosa is hand-shaped and deep-fried to perfection.

What makes a good samosa is the balance between the crunch of the shell and the softness of the filling. At Kumar, we achieve this by controlling the frying temperature precisely and using a dough recipe that Mr. Kumar has refined over decades.

Our samosas are served with two chutneys—tamarind (sweet and tangy) and mint (cool and herbaceous). Together, they create a flavor combination that's both familiar and exotic.

**Order tip:** Samosas are perfect for sharing. Order a plate for the table before your main course.

## #7 — Biryani

**Price: ¥1,600**

Biryani is perhaps the most complex dish in Indian cuisine, and Kumar Restaurant takes it seriously. Our biryani follows the dum pukht method—layers of basmati rice and marinated meat (or vegetables) are sealed in a pot and slow-cooked, allowing the flavors to meld together in ways that quick-cooked dishes simply cannot achieve.

The rice itself is a study in precision. Each grain is long, separate, and infused with saffron, cardamom, and bay leaf. The meat beneath is tender and richly spiced. When the lid is lifted, the aroma alone tells you this dish was worth the wait.

We offer both chicken and vegetable biryani. The vegetable version features seasonal vegetables and is equally complex in its preparation.

**Note:** Biryani is cooked fresh to order, so please allow a few extra minutes for preparation.

## #8 — Paneer Tikka

**Price: ¥1,200**

Paneer tikka is the vegetarian answer to tandoori chicken, and it's every bit as impressive. Cubes of fresh paneer are marinated in spiced yogurt, skewered with bell peppers and onions, and roasted in the tandoor until the edges char and the cheese turns golden.

The tandoor's intense heat creates a smoky, slightly crispy exterior while keeping the paneer inside soft and creamy. It's a textural masterpiece that vegetarian diners adore.

At Kumar, paneer tikka is often the dish that converts skeptics. People who think vegetarian food can't be exciting have clearly never tried it fresh from a tandoor.

**Best paired with:** Mint chutney and a fresh salad.

## #9 — Naan Bread

**Price: ¥250 (plain) / ¥300 (various)**

No Indian meal at Kumar Restaurant is complete without naan. Our naan dough is prepared daily and baked in the tandoor, where it puffs up into a soft, pillowy bread with a lightly charred surface.

We offer several varieties:

- **Plain Naan** — The classic, perfect for mopping up curry sauces
- **Garlic Naan** — Topped with fresh garlic and cilantro
- **Butter Naan** — Brushed with melted butter for extra richness
- **Peshwari Naan** — Filled with a sweet coconut and raisin mixture
- **Keema Naan** — Stuffed with spiced minced lamb

Each type pairs differently with our curries, and our staff is happy to recommend combinations based on what you're ordering.

**Tip:** If you're unsure, garlic naan is the most versatile choice.

## #10 — Gulab Jamun

**Price: ¥400**

Gulab jamun is the crown jewel of Indian desserts. These soft, golden dumplings are made from milk solids and semolina, deep-fried until perfectly golden, then soaked in a warm cardamom-infused sugar syrup.

At Kumar, we make our gulab jamun fresh daily. The dumplings are never pre-made or reheated—they're prepared to order so they arrive at your table warm, soft, and drenched in syrup. The first bite should be a moment: the yielding texture, the sweetness of the syrup, the gentle warmth of cardamom.

Gulab jamun is the traditional ending to any Indian meal, and it's our recommendation for finishing your dining experience on a sweet note.

**Note:** Gulab jamun is served warm. It's best enjoyed immediately.

## Dietary Options at Kumar Restaurant

We believe great food should be accessible to everyone. Here's a quick guide to dietary accommodations:

| Symbol | Meaning | Example Dishes |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| V | Vegetarian | Chana Masala, Palak Paneer, Paneer Tikka |
| VG | Vegan | Vegetable Curry, Chana Masala (without yogurt) |
| H | Halal | All meat dishes |
| GF | Gluten-Free | Rice-based dishes, some curries (ask staff) |

If you have specific dietary needs, please let our staff know when you arrive. We're happy to guide you through the menu and suggest dishes that work for you.
