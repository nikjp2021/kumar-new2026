## Traditional Indian Wedding Elements

Indian weddings are rich with rituals and traditions, each carrying deep symbolic meaning. Here's an overview of the key elements that might be part of your celebration:

**Mehendi Ceremony**

The mehendi ceremony is one of the most visually stunning pre-wedding events. The bride's hands and feet are adorned with intricate henna designs, symbolizing joy, beauty, and spiritual awakening. At Kumar Restaurant, we can help coordinate mehendi artists and create an appropriate setting for this intimate celebration.

**Saptapadi (Seven Steps)**

The saptapadi is the central ritual of a Hindu wedding. The couple takes seven steps together around a sacred fire, each step representing a vow they make to each other. These vows cover nourishment, strength, prosperity, happiness, progeny, long life, and friendship.

This ritual is deeply meaningful and requires careful planning. We work with couples and their priests to ensure the space and timing support the ceremony's significance.

**Mandap Decoration**

The mandap is the canopy under which the wedding ceremony takes place. Traditionally decorated with flowers, fabric, and sometimes mango leaves, the mandap creates a sacred space for the couple's union.

We offer mandap decoration services or can coordinate with external decorators based on your preferences and budget.

**Baraat (Groom's Procession)**

The baraat is the groom's procession to the wedding venue, traditionally accompanied by music, dancing, and celebration. The groom might arrive on a decorated horse, in a vintage car, or on foot—accompanied by family and friends who dance through the streets.

At Kumar Restaurant, we can help coordinate the baraat route and timing, ensuring the groom's arrival is celebrated in style.

## Menu Options for Your Wedding

The wedding menu is one of the most important decisions you'll make. Our culinary team works with each couple to create a menu that reflects their tastes and honors wedding traditions.

**Vegetarian Feast**

A purely vegetarian menu is traditional for many Indian weddings and offers an impressive array of flavors:

- Starters: Samosa, paneer tikka, vegetable pakora
- Main courses: Paneer butter masala, chana masala, dal makhani, vegetable biryani
- Breads: Naan, roti, paratha
- Desserts: Gulab jamun, kheer, jalebi

**Non-Vegetarian Feast**

For couples who want meat dishes alongside vegetarian options:
